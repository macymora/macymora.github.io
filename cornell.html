Bone and Brain Signaling

Research Objectives & Contributions

  • Pioneered a multimodal imaging and computational pipeline to uncover a previously unknown signaling axis between the peripheral nervous system and bone structure, resulting in the validation of a new class of drug targets for anabolic bone therapies.
  • This project delivered a paradigm shift in musculoskeletal research by demonstrating that the body's primary bone repair mechanisms are functionally controlled by the peripheral nervous system. This discovery opens a vital new therapeutic avenue, offering a solution to the limitations of current bone-building drugs
  • I led the discovery and validation of a Neuro-Skeletal Signaling Axis demonstrating that specialized nerve receptors on bone maintenance cells (osteocytes) directly regulate how bone tissue responds to mechanical stress and exercise. This work established a paradigm shift by proving a functional link between neurological pathways and structural bone integrity in a live system.

  • Quantified the mechanical consequences of target disruption using Three-Point Bending to assess whole-bone strength and ductility. This analysis revealed that the loss of normal signaling significantly increased bone failure energy by nearly 100%, indicating a pathological shift toward tissue toughness
