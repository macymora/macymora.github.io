Bone and Brain Signaling

This work introduces a piezo-synchronized imaging technique for removing axial motion artifacts in live bone calcium imaging. The study integrates optical physics, signal processing, and biological validation.

Research Objectives & Contributions

  • Developed real-time motion correction algorithms using mutual information and correlation-based registration.
  • Engineered a piezo-driven imaging setup synchronized with optical acquisition.
  • Demonstrated high temporal fidelity calcium signals in vivo.

Technical Highlights

  • Wrote high-speed acquisition software in MATLAB and Python integrating with Thorlabs and Hamamatsu APIs.
  • Applied signal denoising and multivariate statistical models (PCA, ICA, clustering).
  • Developed automated 3D imaging analysis pipelines for datasets >2 TB.

Motion Correction for Intravital Imaging

A piezo-synchronized imaging approach enabling removal of axial motion artifacts during in vivo bone calcium imaging.

Research Objectives & Contributions

  • Implemented real-time image registration using mutual information.
  • Integrated piezo-driven z-motion correction synchronized with imaging acquisition.
  • Improved fidelity of calcium dynamics in mechanically loaded bone.

Technical Highlights

  • Developed high-speed acquisition software in MATLAB/Python.
  • Applied advanced signal denoising and statistical modeling.
  • Created automated pipelines for large-volume imaging data.
