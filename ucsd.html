Cardiac Growth & Vascular Inflammation

Objectives & Contributions

  • Operated in a multidisciplinary biomedical research environment focused on cardiovascular engineering, stem cell biology, and tissue mechanobiology, contributing to translational research programs.

Technical Highlights

  • Developed in vitro cardiac tissue platforms applying cyclic mechanical stretch to simulate physiological heart contractions and disease-relevant loading conditions, enabling controlled evaluation of cardiac mechanotransduction and functional response.
  • Applied time- and frequency-domain signal processing to electrophysiological ECG data, correlating enhanced electrical activity with upregulated cardiac biomarkers measured via qPCR and Western blot analysis.
  • Collaborated with faculty researchers to advance reproducible, data-backed discovery while implementing wet-lab experimentation, signal analysis, and data interpretation.

Leadership Highlights

  • Selected to present findings at three national conferences and a university-wide symposium, demonstrating exceptional research acumen, persuasive communication, and ability to translate complex data into actionable insights.
  • Secured competitive research funding, including a $3,000 grant, reflecting recognized scientific excellence, strategic project leadership, and tangible translational impact in advancing research objectives.
